🧩 Problem Statement

The existing ERP was feature-rich but suffered from significant usability issues that affected retailers and distributors trying to manage inventory, invoices, and customer records effectively.

Key Issues Identified:

  • •Cluttered interface and poor visual hierarchy
  • •Overwhelming first-time user experience
  • •Too many clicks and scattered workflows
  • •Lack of responsive design for tablets
  • •Accessibility and usability issues for non-technical users

Team Composition

UX/UI Designer (Me) Design lead and heuristic evaluation
Product Manager Task priorities & journey mapping
Frontend Developers (2) Feasibility checks and implementation
Backend Developer Performance impact assessment
QA Tester Edge cases and success state coverage

🚀 Our Goal

To redesign the ERP interface collaboratively, making it intuitive, minimal, efficient, and scalable for retailers and distributors.

✨ We focused on applying UX laws and principles instead of relying only on interviews, as most business logic was already established.

Intuitive Daily Operations

Streamline workflows to make daily inventory, invoice, and customer management tasks effortless and intuitive

Minimal and Modern Design

Create a clean, modern interface that reduces visual clutter and improves focus on essential tasks

Efficient Task Completion

Reduce the number of clicks and steps required to complete core business operations

Scalable Architecture

Design a flexible system that can accommodate new features and growing business needs

Design & Development Process

1

UX Audit & Heuristic Evaluation

Week 1

What We Did

I led a heuristic audit workshop with the product manager and developers, using Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics to evaluate the current platform.

Key Findings

UX Issue UX Law/Heuristic Our Insight
Overloaded top header with 15+ actions Hick's Law More choices = longer decisions
No clear grouping in forms Law of Proximity Related fields scattered
Redundant icons & buttons Jakob's Law Users couldn't rely on familiarity
Table-only product entry Fitts' Law Hard to click; poor mobile experience
No feedback on actions Visibility of System Status Users unsure if tasks were saved
2

Information Architecture & Journey Mapping

Week 2

I collaborated with the PM and two sales staff from the client's side to understand frequent user journeys:

Core User Journeys

  • •Invoice creation (core workflow)
  • •Inventory check and management
  • •Adding new customers
  • •Generating monthly reports

UX Laws Applied

  • •Tesler's Law: Simplified front-end complexity
  • •Serial Position Effect: Prioritized key actions
  • •Progressive Disclosure: Broke down complex tasks
3

Wireframes & Feedback Loop

Week 3

I designed low-fidelity wireframes in notebook, focusing first on Masters, Dashboard overview, and Reports.

Feedback Process

We had 2 synchronous review calls/week (recorded on Zoho), where:

  • →Developers validated feasibility
  • →PM ensured alignment with goals
  • →QA provided insight on edge cases

One great suggestion from QA: Show Simplicity over cluttered interface.

4

High-Fidelity UI & Design System

Week 4-5

Design Decisions

  • •Adopted a 12-column grid layout for responsiveness
  • •Used semantic colors (green = success, yellow = warning, red = error)
  • •Typography levels based on content hierarchy
  • •Custom icon set, following Familiarity Heuristic (Jakob's Law)

Components Created

  • • Global navigation bar
  • • Tabbed invoice form
  • • Product cards with inline editing
  • • Notification & modal system

UX Principles Implementation

Fitts' Law: Enlarged click targets
Miller's Law: Tabbed forms
Feedback: Success/error toasts
WCAG 2.1: 4.5:1 contrast ratio
5

Usability Testing & Final Improvements

Week 6

Though we didn't conduct external testing due to constraints, we conducted internal validation:

  • •Simulated internal user testing using scripts (via Zoom)
  • •Collected async feedback via Notion from client stakeholders

Key Improvement from Feedback

"Added a persistent summary card on the invoice page showing net amount, taxes, and discounts in real-time."

🧭 Learnings & Reflections

🧭
UX Laws Without Formal Research: Even without formal research, UX laws and principles guide meaningful improvements. Heuristic evaluation can reveal critical usability issues efficiently.
🤝
Cross-Role Collaboration: Collaboration across roles ensured technical feasibility and practical decisions. Each team member brought unique insights that improved the final design.
🧩
Design System Early Investment: Creating components and a design system early saved tons of rework and ensured consistency across the entire application.
🗂️
Information Architecture Impact: A clear IA and logical grouping can immediately reduce overwhelm. Proper organization is as important as visual design.

💭 Project Reflection

This ERP redesign project demonstrated the power of systematic UX evaluation and collaborative design. By applying established UX laws and heuristics, we transformed a cluttered, overwhelming interface into an intuitive tool that retailers and distributors could use confidently. The project reinforced that good design doesn't always require extensive user research—sometimes the principles of human-computer interaction provide the guidance needed to create meaningful improvements. Most importantly, working closely with cross-functional team members ensured that our design decisions were both user-centered and technically feasible.
